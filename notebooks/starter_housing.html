Suggested data cleaning actions:

Convert date to a proper datetime and consider extracting year_sold or month_sold as separate features. ​

Check for extreme outliers in price, sqft_living, sqft_lot, and bedrooms (e.g., homes with 0 bedrooms or very large square footage) and decide whether to cap, transform, or remove them. ​

Ensure categorical variables such as zipcode, view, waterfront, grade, condition, and floors are treated as categories (not continuous integers) before modeling. ​

Investigate yr_renovated (many zeros) and possibly create a binary feature like “renovated” plus “age_since_renovation” instead of using the raw year. ​

Check for multicollinearity between highly correlated variables such as sqft_living, sqft_above, and sqft_living15, and consider dropping redundant features for simpler models. ​

Standardize or scale numeric features (especially those with very different ranges like sqft_lot vs. bathrooms) if you plan to use distance-based or regularized models. ​
